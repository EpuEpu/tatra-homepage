We've faced this problem too.

As embedded engineers, we know how often progress stalls — not because of code, but because of access. Physical devices are locked away in labs, full testing can't be done remotely, and AI tools fall short because they can't see what we see: flashing LEDs, moving parts, sensor data, or the invisible communication happening between chips.

So we built our own internal solution first.

We started with a simple need: access our boards from anywhere. We created a remote lab setup — and added tools, including AI-based ones, that already help us work smarter.

We're still improving them every day. And it made us wonder — why isn't this available to everyone?

But what is embedded anyway?

When you walk into a lab and see robots, open devices with circuit boards, blinking lights, and wires everywhere — that's embedded.

It's everything that can't be done with just a laptop and an internet connection. It's real-world hardware. Physical. Tangible. Powerful. And hard to work with remotely.

For business, speed is everything.

Thanks to the AI revolution and no-code tools, building an MVP — like a web service or a SaaS app or mobile app — is faster than ever. In just days, teams can put ideas in front of real users and get valuable feedback.

But companies designing physical products — from smart devices and consumer electronics to robotics and medtech — still need months to create MVP.

We believe it's time to close that gap. R&D teams should be able to experiment faster, test bold ideas sooner — and decide early whether to scale, pivot, or move on to next project.
